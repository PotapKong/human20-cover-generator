# Format Rules

Use this reference before prompting generation. Choose the target format first, then apply the right safe-zone and layout rules.

## 16:9 Dzen / Telegram cover

Goal: strong article thumbnail.

Composition:

- Headline occupies 35-45% of width.
- Visual object/panel occupies 45-55% of width.
- Keep 6-8% safe margins on every side.
- Do not place critical text near edges.
- Use 1 main metaphor.
- Small Human 2.0 / H2.0 brand mark in bottom-left or top-left safe zone.
- Optional dot pattern in top-right.

Best for:

- article cover
- Telegram post cover
- Dzen thumbnail
- horizontal social banner

## 9:16 Instagram / Reels cover

Goal: readable on Instagram profile, feed, and Reels grid.

Composition:

- Headline in the top 35-45%.
- Main visual in the middle 35-45%.
- Brand footer in the bottom safe zone.
- Keep central crop safe: key text must fit inside the middle 80% width and 70% height.
- Avoid wide horizontal diagrams.
- Use vertical stack: headline -> visual -> brand.
- Keep the headline to 3-5 short lines.
- Do not place essential content in the extreme top/bottom where app UI may cover it.

Best for:

- Reels cover
- Instagram vertical cover
- Shorts/TikTok-style visual

## 4:3 / 1:1 infographic

Goal: compact explanatory slide.

Composition:

- One headline.
- 2-4 cards max.
- No tiny source lines unless user asks.
- Prefer icons and numbers over paragraphs.
- Use Human 2.0 palette and white card surfaces.
- Keep a consistent grid and readable spacing.

Best for:

- carousel slides
- concept diagrams
- simple comparison tables
- data cards

## Narrow horizontal infographic

Goal: readable strip for article body or carousel detail.

Composition:

- Use 3-5 statistic cards or one clean timeline.
- Keep card titles short.
- Use large numbers.
- Add source line only if it remains readable.
- Avoid vertical stacking that makes the strip too tall.
- Keep brand mark small and unobtrusive.

## Outpainting / crop repair

When user says the image is cut off or needs padding:

- Preserve the original infographic exactly.
- Extend the background around it.
- Add clean white/light-gray margin.
- Do not redesign the inner content.
- Do not rewrite existing text unless requested.
- Maintain brand dots/lines if they continue naturally.
